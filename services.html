Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreign citizens buy property in Morocco?

Yes, foreign citizens have the absolute right to purchase real estate in Morocco (apartments, villas, commercial spaces). The only restriction is for non-agricultural land outside city limits. We guide foreign investors seamlessly through the 100% legal process, including opening Moroccan convertible bank accounts.

What are the typical notary and tax fees when buying?

In general, closing costs (taxes, land registry, notary fees) amount to approximately 6% to 7% of the property's purchase price. We provide a full transparent breakdown before you sign any commitments, ensuring zero hidden costs.

How much do you charge for your services?

For property sales, our standard agency fee is 2.5% (+ VAT) from the buyer and 2.5% (+ VAT) from the seller, which is the regulated standard in Morocco. For property management, we charge a flat 10% fee on collected rents. Initial consultations and valuations are always 100% free.
